**Lesson goal:** Use the Interactive Guitar Fretboard + Piano Trainer to see, hear, and understand G Ionian (the G major scale).

## 2. Set G Ionian

Open the trainer—again, link in the description. You'll see a fretboard, a piano, and some controls at the top.

We're going to set it up for G Ionian. Ionian is degree 1—the same pattern as the major scale.

First, leave Type on Mode. Then set Tonic to G. In the Degree dropdown, choose 1 – Ionian.

[PAUSE – click Tonic: G, Degree: 1 – Ionian.]

The header will say something like "Key of G, Mode: Ionian." Now the fretboard and piano are showing only the seven notes of the G major scale: G, A, B, C, D, E, F sharp, and back to G. Those are the only notes in G Ionian.

[SHOW: Fretboard and piano.]

---

## 3. What we're seeing

Before we click anything, here's what's on the screen.

The yellow dots are degree 1—the root, G. The purple dots are degree 3—the third of the scale. The green dots are degree 5—the fifth. The gray dots are the other scale degrees: 2, 4, 6, and 7. So you're seeing which scale degree each note is, not just the note name.

The same idea is on the piano below: only the notes of G Ionian are highlighted. Everything is derived from your choices: tonic G and degree 1. Change the tonic or the degree, and the whole picture updates.

---

## 4. Hear the scale with Play

Let's hear the scale in order. Click Play.

[Click Play.]

The trainer will play the seven notes of G Ionian in order, from G up to the next G. You'll hear it ascend so your ear gets the sound of the scale. The note panel and the dots will highlight each note as it plays. That's G Ionian: one octave, root to root.

[Let sequence finish.]

---

## 5. Click a fret

Now make it interactive. Click any yellow dot on the fretboard—any G.

[Click a G.]

You'll hear that G, and the note panel will show you the note and that it's degree 1. Click a purple dot—that's degree 3, the third of the scale. Click a green dot—degree 5, the fifth. Every dot you click plays the note and tells you its scale degree. So you're not just learning where the note is; you're learning what role it has in G Ionian.

[Optional: click B and D, point out note panel.]

If you want to quiz yourself, you can switch Display to "Reveal on click": the note names hide until you click. Great for testing whether you know the degrees.

---

## 6. Why this matters

G Ionian is your roadmap for the key of G. Melodies, chords, and licks in G major use these seven notes. The trainer doesn't hardcode "G major"—it builds everything from tonic G and degree 1. So when you change to another mode or another key, the same logic applies: you pick the tonic and the degree, and the tool shows you the right notes and degrees on the neck and the piano.
